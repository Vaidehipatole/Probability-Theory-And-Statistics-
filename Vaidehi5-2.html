INTRODUCTION

Correlation:
Correlation is a statistical measure that expresses the degree to which two variables are linearly connected (meaning they change together at a constant rate). Its a typical approach for describing simple relationships without stating cause and effect. Correlations are useful for describing simple data correlations. Consider the following scenario: you are looking at a dataset of campsites in a mountain park. Youre curious if theres a link between the elevation of the campsite (how high up the mountain it is) and the typical high temperature in the summer. You have two measurements for each campsite: height and temperature. When you use a correlation to examine these two variables across your sample, you can establish a linear relationship: as elevation increases, temperature decreases. They are inversely connected.

Simple linear regression:
To evaluate the association between two quantitative variables, simple linear regression is performed. You can use simple linear regression when you want to know:

  1. The strength of the link between two variables (e.g.the relationship between rainfall and soil erosion).

  2. At a given value of the independent variable, the value of the dependent variable (e.g.the amount of soil erosion at a certain level of rainfall).

Example You are a social scientist who is interested in the connection between income and happiness. You poll 500 people with earnings ranging from $15k to $75k and ask them to rate their level of happiness on a scale of 1 to 10. Because your independent variable (income) and dependent variable (happiness) are both quantitative, you can perform a regression analysis to check if they have a linear connection.

History:
Francis Galton invented correlation in late 1888, when he noticed a common thread in three different scientific topics he was examining. Karl Pearsons widely known linkage of correlation with a retreat into a recess at Naworth Castle is addressed and contrasted with Galtons own 1890 version of the time of discovery. The events that prompted Galton to write the account are discussed.

Practical Application In Industry:
Correlation is frequently used in portfolio management (Portfolio management is the art and science of selecting and overseeing a group of investments that meet the long-term financial objectives and risk tolerance of a client, a company, or an institution) to assess the degree of diversification among the assets in a portfolio. To help establish the most optimal frontier, modern portfolio theory (MPT) (The modern portfolio theory (MPT) is a practical method for selecting investments in order to maximize their overall returns within an acceptable level of risk) employs a measure of the correlation of all the assets in a portfolio. This notion aids in the optimization of expected returns versus a given amount of risk. Including assets with low correlation to one another helps to lower overall risk in a portfolio. Nonetheless, correlation can shift over time. It can only be measured in the past. Two assets that have previously had a high degree of connection can become uncorrelated and begin to move independently. This is one of MPTs flaws; it presupposes constant relationships between assets.

Observation
The correlation coefficient is a statistical measure of the strength of the association between two variables relative movements. The values range from -1.0 to 1.0. A computed value greater than 1.0 or less than -1.0 indicates that the correlation measurement was incorrect. A correlation of -1.0 indicates that there is a perfect negative connection, whereas a correlation of 1.0 indicates that there is a perfect positive correlation. A correlation of 0.0 indicates that there is no linear link between the two variables movements.

The correlation coefficientin this scenario suggests a strong relationship between displacement and cylinders of the car. There may be a direct cause-and-effect relationship between the variables.

The R2 score, also known as the coefficient of determination, is used to evaluate the efficacy of a linear regression model. The amount of variance in the output dependent characteristic that can be predicted based on the input independent variable (s). Its used to examine how effectively the model reproduces observed findings, based on the ratio of total deviation of results explained by the model.

In this current scenario, R2 = 0.865 ;86 percent ofthevariation of displacement isduetothe variation of cylinders; 14% is due to chance
